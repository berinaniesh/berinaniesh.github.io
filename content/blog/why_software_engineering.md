## Introduction

If you have read my [about page](/about), you would know that I have a master's degree in nuclear engineering and a bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering and yet I chose software engineering as my career path. Some people, especially my parents just can't wrap their heads around this fact and whenever they ask me why I chose this field, I find it really hard to explain to them in a few words. This blog post attempts to answer that question once and for all. 

## One Word Answer (Open Source)

Let's forget the skill necessary, but imagine an easily accessible place, where you can go to get the blueprint, see the design process, get all required technical documents and specifications and also read the log of each and every job done by every engineer when building an Airbus 320 engine. Now you get all documents and tools necessary to build an engine and start building an engine in your backyard (atleast try).
